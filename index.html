The Intermission Orchestra

December Concert

Anime, Video Game, and Film Music

About the Event

The Intermission Orchestra (also known as TIO) is BACK with another concert on December 9th, 2025. Bring your friends and family to First Congregational Church at Berkeley for an exciting and FREE night of music from your favorite anime, video games, and movies. Whether you're a student that needs a break from locking in for finals, or you're a pop culture fan in the greater Bay Area, THIS is the concert for you!

What to Expect

A night full of fun music from your favorite anime, video games, and films for FREE!

  • Live Music: Arranged by members within the orchestra! Our members have worked hard throughout the semester in rehearsals to bring you a magical performance!
  • Art Gallery: Admire the beautiful visual art our art staff members have created throughout the semester!
  • Sticker and Keychain Booth: Stop by and purchase hand-drawn stickers and keychains made by our members! Sales help support our mission to keep all of our concerts entirely free to the public!

Event Schedule

7:30PM - Doors Open!

Enter through the main entrance of First Congregational Church at Berkeley.

Please avoid parking in the church parking lot as staff will lock the space before the end of the concert. Alternate parking options can be found both on-campus and off-campus.

You will be expected to show your (FREE) entry ticket on your device to an usher before taking your seat! Ushers will guide Priority Admission ticket attendees to their seats in the front. Please note there is additional seating available upstairs on the second floor.

8:00PM - Performance Starts

Sit back and relax as our orchestra serenades you! There will be a 15 minute intermission around the halfway point of the concert to stretch your legs, use the restroom, purchase merch from our artist table in the lobby, and chat with orchestra members!

10:00PM - Performance Ends

Please give orchestra members some time to pack up their things and help put away music stands and chairs before greeting them.
